The Beginning of the End

Cretaceous–Paleogene (K–Pg) extinction event was a sudden mass extinction of three-quarters of the plant and animal species on Earth, which occurred approximately 66 million years ago

The asteroid impact devastated the global environment, mainly through a lingering impact winter which halted photosynthesis in plants and plankton

Furthermore, the Deccan Traps and other volcanic eruptions, climate change, and sea level change may have further contributed to the extinction of dinosaurs.

A wide range of species perished, the best-known being the non-avian dinosaurs. It also destroyed myriad other terrestrial organisms, including some mammals, birds, lizards, insects, plants, and all the pterosaurs

In the oceans, the K–Pg extinction killed off plesiosaurs and mosasaurs and devastated teleost fish, sharks, mollusks, and many species of plankton

This devastation that happened 66 Million Years ago provided us with fossil fuel and natural resources which helped fuel the rapid advancement of our society. And for that, we are forever grateful.
