Triceratops

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Triceratops horridus

DIET: Herbivore

SIZE: About 30 feet long

WEIGHT: Six to eight tons

With its three sharp horns and spiky head plate, Triceratops horridus must have been an intimidating presence as it trampled across western North America. Triceratops, which is Latin for "three-horned face," was among the last non-avian dinosaurs to evolve before the cataclysmic extinction event that occurred 66 million years ago. Furthermore, Triceratops is one of the most abundant dinosaurs unearthed by paleontologists. While there are no evidence suggesting that Triceratops waged epic battles against Tyrannosaurus rex, the Triceratop skulls reveal that these dinosaurs did sometimes fight one another. And unlike other ceratopsian species, Triceratops fossils are rarely found in groups, suggesting they lived solitary lives.
